November 1, 2015 Jane Doe

So it's been a week and a half since I've started my internship here at SK Hynix. For those of you don't know, SK Hynix is a South Korean Semiconductor company that specializes in DRAM and NAND Flash memory technology. So bascially the company makes RAM(DRAM) and SSDs(NAND Flash). As for me, I applied for the Solution SW team in the eSSD sector. The company has supplied its SSDs primarily to enterprises, hence the name eSSD. Compared to SSDs sold to consumers (cSSDs), eSSDs are designed to fit enterprise needs such as Data Centers, Cloud storage and etc. Hence, they need to be less error prone and faster. eSSDs compose of single-level-cells (SLCs) which stores a single bit of information per cell. On the other hand, Multi-level-cells(MLCs) or Triple-level-cells(TLCs) used in cSSD, are able to store more data (2bits, 3bits respectively), but are slower to read and write, and are more error prone. Enough with the explaining, I work with the Solution SW team in the eSSD sector. Specifically, the team builds and develops the software that goes in between the host(PC) and the SSD. Without the layer of software in between, the host is not able to make effective use of the memory space in SSD. Hence, this layer of interface really does the remapping to efficiently read/write data back and forth between the host and the SSD. Here is where it gets interesting. The layer of interface follows the a universal standard called the NVM Express. Many of you may of heard of this term, as it often tags along in the name of the SSD product as "NVMe". The NVMe organization has been working on a new technology to add to the existing NVMe called Zone Namespace. This technology effectively eliminates any garbage collection that had been caused previously when writing and reading SSDs. This is a huge addition to the existing technology, as it improves read and write space by up to 20%, and significantly reduces the wasted memory which was previously allocated to storing garbage collection. Unfortunately, the spec sheet for the ZNS technology is not public yet, and I only have access to it inside the company. For the 6 week of my internship, I get to mingle with this new upcoming technology. As NVMe has yet to supply SK Hynix with the new software interface, I was given the task to emulate a ZNS device for the team to work with.
